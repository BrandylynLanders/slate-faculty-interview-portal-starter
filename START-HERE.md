# START HERE

If you only read one file, read this.

## What you are building

You are NOT just building a portal.

You are building a way to:
- bring faculty into your process  
- reduce spreadsheets and email coordination  
- ensure interview data is captured inside Slate  

---

## What a “portal” means (in plain terms)

A portal is simply:
> a controlled view into Slate for a specific group of users

In this case:
- Faculty log in
- See only what they need
- Complete interviews and evaluations
- Never need full Slate access

---

## Key idea

A portal is not extra work.

It is how you:
- make your system usable for others  
- increase adoption  
- reduce manual coordination  

---

## Before you build anything

Do NOT start in Slate.

Start with planning:
Go to **1-Plan-Your-Process.md**

---

## What a basic version includes

You do NOT need everything at once.

A simple version can include:
- One interview event  
- One scheduler setup  
- One evaluation form  
- One portal view showing assigned interviews  

That is enough to get started.

---

## How to use this guide

Work in this order:

1. Plan Your Process  
2. Build Your Foundation  
3. Build the Faculty Portal  
4. Add Forms  
5. Connect Events and Scheduler  
6. Add Reporting  

---

## Final note

You do not need a perfect system.

You need:
- a clear process  
- a small starting point  
- and a way to bring people into Slate  

Start small. Improve over time.
